Sustainable Development Goal 11

Make cities inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable

Cities are hubs for ideas, commerce, culture, science, productivity, social development and much more. At their best, cities have enabled people to advance socially and economically.

However, many challenges exist to maintaining cities in a way that continues to create jobs and prosperity while not straining land and resources. Common urban challenges include congestion, lack of funds to provide basic services, a shortage of adequate housing and declining infrastructure.

The challenges cities face can be overcome in ways that allow them to continue to thrive and grow, while improving resource use and reducing pollution and poverty. The future we want includes cities of opportunities for all, with access to basic services, energy, housing, transportation and more.

Our View:

We bring together Diplomats and international students at various events, allowing Diplomats to share their experience and knowledge with our CI students. The events we host have a doubly positive effect; they bring together other organizations to be part of our initiatives, and they engage the international community on an individual level. In doing so, we have gained significant support from educational institutions across New York City, United Nations Agencies, Embassies, and Missions to United Nations. We have established a strong partnership with the New York City council on social issues. We are establishing a strong relationship with the New York City Mayors Youth Envoy on matters of youth and students. By using the diversity of New York City to our advantage, we have created a group that strives for equal and fair access to basic services and needs. This brings us in line with SDG 11. Our planned trip to Toronto, Canada, will be an additional insurance of our commitment to SDG 11.
