Sustainable Development Goal 16

Promote just, peaceful and inclusive societies

Goal 16 of the Sustainable Development Goals is dedicated to the promotion of peaceful and inclusive societies for sustainable development, the provision of access to justice for all, and building effective, accountable institutions at all levels.

Our View:

The youth population (people between the ages of 13-21) are such a large segment of the overall population that they must play a significant role in nation- building and creating a more just, peaceful and inclusive world. Collegiate Internationals (CI) views SDG 16 as a fundamental base of all its objectives. CI plays an important role in bringing together students from all walks of life. The team of Collegiate Internationals is made up of students from all over the world. We make sure that their voices are heard.

Peace is the primary objective. Our vision for a peaceful society is possible only if we understand any deficits in our communities, and then rectify them. Our research has found that the cause of unrest and tension is a lack of knowledge about other cultures and traditions. CI believes that it is possible to achieve peace in a globalized world through educating students and youth about other nationâ€™s cultures and commonalities. We thus invite UN Diplomats and global leaders for a non-political, academic forum: The Diplomatic Forum. This gives young people a chance to learn from and about other nations in a way that fosters similarities and understanding, allowing for young people to help build a more peaceful world.

UN Target:

  • Significantly reduce all forms of violence and related death rates everywhere

  • End abuse, exploitation, trafficking and all forms of violence against and torture of children

  • Promote the rule of law at the national and international levels and ensure equal access to justice for all

  • By 2030, significantly reduce illicit financial and arms flows, strengthen the recovery and return of stolen assets and combat all forms of organized crime

  • Substantially reduce corruption and bribery in all their forms

  • Develop effective, accountable and transparent institutions at all levels

  • Ensure responsive, inclusive, participatory and representative decision-making at all levels

  • Broaden and strengthen the participation of developing countries in the institutions of global governance

  • By 2030, provide legal identity for all, including birth registration

  • Ensure public access to information and protect fundamental freedoms, in accordance with national legislation and international agreements

  • Strengthen relevant national institutions, including through international cooperation, for building capacity at all levels, in particular in developing countries, to prevent violence and combat terrorism and crime

  • Promote and enforce non-discriminatory laws and policies for sustainable development
